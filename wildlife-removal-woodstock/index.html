Hear movement in the attic or chimney? We check both, remove the wildlife, and close the opening it used.

Woodstock has thick tree cover from Towne Lake to the Noonday Creek trail. Older streets and newer neighborhoods sit close together. Squirrels and raccoons can move from branches to roofs, vents, and chimney caps.

Tree cover meets the roof in Woodstock

A branch over the gutter can act like a bridge. Squirrels may chew near a vent. Raccoons can test a chimney cap or a loose part of the roof.

The mix of old and new homes matters. An older house may have worn wood. A newer house may have a small build gap hidden under the eave.

The sound alone does not tell the whole story. We look at the time of day, marks near the opening, and the space below it before we choose a removal plan.

From chimney check to clean space

A chimney call is not the same as a wall or attic call. We start with the exact place you heard the noise, then widen the check.

1. Read the signs

We inspect the chimney, roof, attic, and nearby tree-side entry points.

2. Get wildlife out

We remove or trap the animal with humane methods and no poisons.

3. Seal the access

We close chewed gaps, loose edges, and other openings that wildlife could use.

4. Clear the mess

We clean and disinfect the area where nesting or waste was found.

Our team is licensed by the GA DNR and the GA Department of Agriculture. We use humane methods. We do not use poisons.
